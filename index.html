Noam Chomsky

The Father of Modern Linguistics

Here is a timeline of Noam Chomsky's life:

  • 1928 - Born Avram Noam Chomsky in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.
  • 1945 - Teaches Hebrew to fund his education at University of Pennsylvania.
  • 1951 - Recieves M.A after studying theoretical linguistics while developing an interest in philosophy.
  • 1955 - Awarded Ph.D and started as an assistant professor at M.I.T.
  • 1962 - Became involved in active political protest against the Vietnam War.
  • 1979 - Published a revised version of The Political Economy of Human Rights.
  • 1988 - Published Manufacturing Consent: The Political Economy of the Mass Media.
  • 1999 - Helped East Timor achieve independence from Indonesia.
  • 2015 - Announced support for Bernie Sanders in the 2016 United States Presidential Election.
  • 2016 - Summarized his views on capitalism and economic inequality in the documentary Requiem for the American Dream.
